The Go-Bag is designed to sustain **one person for 72 hours (3 days)** while they are actively evacuating or "bugging out" to a safer location. It should be packed and ready to grab at a moment's notice.

| Category | Essential Items | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Water** | 3 liters (or filtering straw/tablets), metal water bottle. | Hydration is paramount for survival. |
| **Food** | 3 days of non-cook, high-calorie food (e.g., energy bars, MREs). | Provides energy without needing utilities. |
| **Shelter** | Tarp, rope, mylar emergency blanket, compact sleeping bag. | Protection from the elements (Rule of 3 Hours). |
| **Fire** | Lighter, waterproof matches, ferro rod, tinder. | Signaling, warmth, water purification, morale. |
| **First Aid** | Comprehensive kit, required personal medications (3-7 days supply). | Treating injuries and maintaining health. |
| **Tools** | Multi-tool or knife, headlamp, duct tape, compass, whistle. | Utility, navigation, and signaling for help. |
| **Security** | Self-defense item (where legal), cash (small bills). | Personal safety and means of transaction if ATMs fail. |

## ðŸ’¼ The Get-Home-Bag (GHB)

The GHB is specifically designed to get you from work or another remote location **back to your primary home**. It is smaller, lighter, and focused on mobility and overcoming obstacles.

* **Location:** Kept in your vehicle, office, or carried in a backpack daily.
* **Focus:** Walking/hiking gear, small medical kit, small water filter, reflective vest, sturdy shoes (if you don't wear them daily), and navigation tools.

## ðŸ  The Stay-Put Kit (Home Supply)

This is your main storage, intended to sustain your **entire household for a minimum of two weeks (ideally 3+ months)** in a shelter-in-place scenario.

* **Water:** **Minimum 1 gallon per person, per day** for 14 days (or $14 \times \text{People}$ gallons). Include extra for hygiene.
* **Food:** Deep pantry of shelf-stable meals, dry goods, pet food, and comfort foods.
* **Sanitation:** Extra garbage bags, bleach (unscented), buckets for waste, personal hygiene supplies.
* **Power/Heat:** Firewood, backup generator fuel, battery bank, and a means of cooking indoors without power (e.g., propane stove, *used with extreme ventilation*).
* **Paperwork:** Securely stored copies of vital documents (passports, insurance, deeds) in a fireproof/waterproof container.

> **ACTION ITEM:** Assemble a basic Go-Bag today. Even a simple backpack with water, a blanket, and a flashlight is a huge step forward.
